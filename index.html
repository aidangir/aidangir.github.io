Cars and What to Look for

    The majority of cars on the road are too complicated and unreliable for car owners. Range Rovers are a common example of a car brand that brakes down often. KIAs are also a common complaint. Car brands that are more reliable for the typical car owner include Toyotas and Hondas.

For car enthusiasts, an Audi or Mustang would be a more fun option. They have a decent mix of reliability and sportiness. Specific aspects of sportiness for Mustangs include a manual transmission and rear-wheel drive, with a loud high-revving V8. For Audis, owners enjoy daily drivability with off-the-line quickness.

I've been working on cars from a young age, and I think a more in-depth knowledge of cars will help individuals make more informed decisions about what to drive on the road.

       
  
    

Toyota

      HOW TOYOTA STANDS OUT:
Unbelievable reliability. Drivers don't have to worry about the drivetrain of the vehicle for at least 300,000 miles.

COMMON PROBLEMS: Aside from easily rusting frames on some trucks, there are no common problems with Toyotas. Some would consider boring Toyota bodies a con.

REASONS I WOULD RECOMMEND IT: I would never worry about it in terms of reliability. I know it would always work.      

   
  
    

Honda

    HOW HONDA STANDS OUT:
Very good reliability. They're a little less boring than Toyotas.

COMMON PROBLEMS: Some of the electronics in the newer vehicles fail rather quickly.

REASONS I WOULD RECOMMEND IT: Solid daily drivers. Gives you everything you need.     

  
  
    

Mustang

    HOW MUSTANG STANDS OUT:
Pretty solid reliability with over 430hp engine, made into a manual transmission and rear-wheel drive, making it into one hell of a fun car.

COMMON PROBLEMS: Sometimes the manual transmission can act up.

REASONS I WOULD RECOMMEND IT: Horsepower to price ratio is really good. They're super solid vehicles.     

  
  
    

Audi

    HOW AUDI STANDS OUT:
They're really good daily drivers that are sporty and fun. Their audio systems are fire.

COMMON PROBLEMS: Can be really complicated and/or expensive to fix.

REASONS I WOULD RECOMMEND IT: It's a daily driver that's fun and decently reliable.
